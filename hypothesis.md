# HYPOTHESIS - Track A: The Concept Check

## What I observed (Move 1)

I sat with two cohort-mates and asked them to explain from
scratch why a backend exists. Both could DEFINE the concept
confidently. But neither could DERIVE it from first principles
on the first try.

Both gave memorized-sounding answers ("it acts as a filter",
"security breaches and breakdowns") and both needed exactly
2 follow-up questions before they reached the real reasoning
(browser code is visible to anyone through developer tools).

Same concept, same gap, same number of pushes to close it.
Two different people.

## The Claim

A Cohort 7 learner who can define a systems concept (like why a
backend exists or what an API is) will fail to derive it from
first principles when pushed with a sharp follow-up question.
They will fall back on a memorized phrase before they can reason
through it themselves.

However, when the specific gap is pointed out and they are pushed
with a targeted follow-up, most will be able to close the gap
and reach real understanding.

## Concept under test

"Why does a backend exist at all? Why can't we just run
everything in the browser?"

## Result that proves me right

2 out of 2 Move 5 users fail to derive on their first attempt
(they give a memorized phrase), but at least 1 out of 2 closes
the gap after the tool gives them a targeted follow-up.

## Result that means no problem exists

Both Move 5 users derive the concept cleanly on their first try
with no memorized phrases and no gap to find. The tool has
nothing to expose.

## Kill number (I stop defending the idea and admit I was wrong)

If both Move 5 users (including the cold user) explain the
concept from first principles on their very first attempt,
with no gap sentence detected and no follow-up needed, then
the problem I am testing for does not exist and my hypothesis
was wrong. I will report this honestly.
